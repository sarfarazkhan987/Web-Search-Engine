The program bridges two main scholarly approaches to the study of communication, media, and culture – political economy and critical cultural studies. It is designed to advance original and provocative interpretations of everyday communicative and symbolic environments; facilitate research on the definition, evolution, and transformation of rights and communicative practices, whether through policy, culture, and/or custom; and develop practices and strategies oriented toward social justice initiatives. Topics of Interest Ownership and control of media, information technology and globalization Communication networks, new media, alternative media, and social justice movements Cultural production, consumption, identity, politics and practices of “representation” (class, gender, race, ethnicity, sexuality, etc.), and popular culture Commodification of information, culture and communication Program Specific Admission Requirements Deadline for Fall: January 15 Minimum TOEFL: IBT-100 Minimum IELTS: 7.0 Additional Requirements Statement of purpose (approx. 500 words) Curriculum Vitae (CV) or Resume Writing sample (10-15 pages) Two academic letters of reference General admission requirements for all Graduate Programs Scholarships and Funding Learn More Request More InformationGraduate Academic Calendar Program and Course DescriptionsVisit Communication, Media, and Film Apply to this Program share tweet save Grad News 2020 Trudeau Foundation Doctoral Scholarships - Call for nominations OGS & QEII-GSST Competition 2020/21 Autism Scholars Award 2020-21 Ontario Women's Health Scholars Awards 2020-21 OGS/QEII-GSST and CGS-M info session - copy of materials More News Future Students FAQ How do I apply?
