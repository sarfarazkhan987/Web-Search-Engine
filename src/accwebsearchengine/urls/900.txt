Each month, we invite a different guest speaker to come and share his/her experiences in how he/she incorporates entrepreneurship in his/her course in the entrepreneurship stream. In commercialization stream, the guest speaker will share with the audience his/her business and how that business supports his/her research and vice versa. Members from all faculties are welcome to attend. September 2019 Topic: How a law professor incorporates entrepreneurship into her course Guest Speaker: Professor Myra Tawfik, Professor of Law and EPICentre Professor of IP Commercialization & Strategy Description: Professor Tawfik, in partnership with EPICentre, launched an experiential learning course for her law students called the “EPICentre Practicum” in Winter 2019 semester. Professor Tawfik will share her experiences in how this idea came along and how the course works. Where: EPICentre workshop room When: Friday, September 27th, 2019 from 12:00 – 1:00 pm To register, rsvp to Wen Teoh at wteoh@uwindsor.ca before September 20th, 2019. October 2019 Topic: How an engineering professor use commercialization strategy to support her research Guest Speaker: Dr. Jill Urbanic, Professor of Mechanical, Automotive & Materials Engineering Description: Dr. Jill Urbanic co-founded CAMufacturing Solutions in 2015 as she saw the need of her expertise in customized manufacturing software in the industry. Find out more how her company plays a role in supporting her research in her Advanced Production and Design Lab. Where: EPICentre workshop room When: Thursday, October 31st, 2019 from 12:00 – 1:00 pm To register, rsvp to Wen Teoh at wteoh@uwindsor.ca before October 24th, 2019. November 2019 Topic: How an art professor teaches her students entrepreneurial skills Guest Speaker: Dr. Jennifer Willet, Associate Professor, School of Creative Arts & Director, INCUBATOR Art Lab Description: Dr. Jennifer Willet, Canada Research Chair in Art, Science and Ecology, will discuss her strategies for integrating students into her professional research and artistic activities. Through her undergraduate bioart class, the MFA program in Visual Arts, and the INCUBATOR Art Lab research group, students gain first hand experience in the entrepreneurial aspects of producing arts events, and managing an art career. Where: EPICentre workshop room When: Thursday, November 28th, 2019 from 12:00 – 1:00 pm To register, rsvp to Wen Teoh at wteoh@uwindsor.ca before November 21st, 2019.
